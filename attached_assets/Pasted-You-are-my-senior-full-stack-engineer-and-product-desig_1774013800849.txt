## Critical Safety Rules
These rules are mandatory and must shape the architecture from day one:

### 1) Public map safety
- Never expose exact GPS coordinates of vulnerable people publicly.
- Need posts from displaced people or families must use approximate district-level or area-level map pins only.
- Example: if a user says they are in Hamra, the public marker must appear in central Hamra or a fuzzed district point, not their exact building.
- Exact private addresses, if collected, must never be shown publicly.

### 2) Offers vs requests map logic
- Need posts = approximate / fuzzed location only
- NGO and public aid centers = exact or semi-exact location allowed
- Individual providers = approximate by default unless explicitly public

### 3) Freshness / expiration
- Requests and offers must not stay active forever.
- Every post must include:
  - createdAt
  - updatedAt
  - expiresAt
  - status
  - lastConfirmedAt
- Stale / expired posts must be hidden from the public map by default.

### 4) Trust / moderation
- Every post must have a “Report this post” action.
- NGOs may have a manually assigned verified badge.
- Do not build full identity verification yet.
- Add basic admin moderation support from the beginning.
